Getting started
Readiness assessment

Every community is different. Before deployment, we run a largely offline discovery engagement to assess the state of readiness: infrastructure, connectivity, language coverage, and existing data systems. This lets us customize a solution that interfaces cleanly with our platform and data schema.

Offline work. Self-guided exercises and assessments completed by the community at their own pace.

The ecosystem
What we're building together

Ani is phase 1. The full GFFTS ecosystem is a circular food economy where every participant benefits: farmers earn more, consumers pay less, waste becomes value, and the environment regenerates. Forum members shape what gets built next.

Produce → Distribute → Consume → Regenerate → Repeat

OmniFresh logistics

Smart routing, fast delivery, reverse logistics. Nothing gets stuck, nothing spoils.

PHASE 2

Marketplace + barter

Trade, swap, buy, sell. Cash, barter, and GFFTS credits all accepted. Inclusive by design.

PHASE 2

Rewards + impact

Earn credits for reducing waste, buying local, planting mangroves. Behavior that heals the system gets rewarded.

PHASE 3

Waste recovery + recycling

Food rescue for near-spoiling goods. Composting. Packaging reuse. AI detects waste before it happens and redirects it to value.

PHASE 3

Subscriptions + stability

Weekly food baskets. Price protection. Guaranteed demand for farmers, guaranteed supply for families. The stability layer that makes everything predictable.

Why this matters now

Globalization is ending. AI is accelerating. The nations that feed themselves will lead the next century.

Supply chains are fracturing. Climate events are intensifying. And the communities that grow the world's food still can't access the intelligence systems they need. GFFTS exists to close that gap, with AI that works where it's needed most, not where it's easiest to build.

How it works
From assessment to impact

Deploying Ani in your community starts with understanding where you are. Every engagement follows the same proven path.

1
Assess

4-week offline readiness engagement. We evaluate infrastructure, connectivity, language needs, and existing data systems.

2

3

Train community champions, distribute devices, and go live. Farmers start reporting harvests by voice on day one.

Scale

Real-time dashboards drive policy. AI recommendations improve outcomes. Expand to neighboring communities.

The founder
Glorianne Montefrio

Founder, GFFTS • Impact Architect • Mindanao native

Glorianne grew up in Iligan, Mindanao. She spent nearly two decades building businesses and managing large-scale projects internationally before returning to the Philippines to launch GFFTS and bring AI-powered food systems to the communities she grew up in.

She has spoken at the World Corporate Summit, Step Dubai, and regional innovation forums. She understands the language of international funders and the reality of Mindanaoan communities, because she has lived in both worlds.

GFFTS exists because Glorianne believes the communities that feed the world deserve the same AI tools as the corporations that profit from them. Ani is not a side project. It is the reason she is coming home.

"I did not grow up in a boardroom. I grew up in Mindanao. I know what it feels like to leave, and to want to give something real back."

The vision
From Mindanao to the G20

Ani is designed to work anywhere farmers grow food, communities lack connectivity, and governments struggle to reach their people. The model is open. The playbook is free. The mission scales.

2026 – Philippines

50 communities in Mindanao. B'laan and T'boli indigenous communities first. Prove the model. Collect the evidence. Publish the guide.

2027 – Southeast Asia

Expand to Visayas, Luzon highlands. First international pilots in Indonesia and East Timor with local partner organizations.

2028+ – G20 nations

Open-source model available to any organization globally. Target: agricultural communities across South Asia, Sub-Saharan Africa, and Latin America.
